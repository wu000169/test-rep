Saturn

Adorned with thousands of beautiful ringlets, Saturn is unique among the planets. All four gas giant planets have rings --made of chunks of ice and rock --but none are as spectacular or as complicated as Saturn's. Like the other gas giants, Saturn is mostly a massive ball of hydrogen and helium.

Missions from Earth

  1. Pioneer Ⅰ

    2. Pioneer ⅠⅠ, the sister spacecraft to Pioneer 1, was the first human-made object to fly past Saturn and also returned the first pictures of the polar regions of Jupiter. Pioneer ⅠⅠ detected Saturn's bow shock on 31 August 1979, about 1.5 million kilometers out from the planet, thus providing the first conclusive evidence of the existence of Saturn's magnetic field. The spacecraft crossed the plane beyond the outer ring at 14:36 UT on 1 September 1979 and then passed by the planet at 16:31 UT for a chose encounter at a range of 20,900 kilometers. It was moving at a relative speed of 114,100 kilometers per hour at the point of closest approach.

  2. Voyager 1

    3. Voyager 1 is one of two spacecraft exploring where nothing from Earth has flown before. Launched in 1997, the mission's primary science targets were Jupiter and Satur. Voyager 1 has been observing the interplanetary medium throughout its journey, and is now in interstellar space, the region between the stars. Voyager 1 was to become the second spacecraft to visit Saturn. Its mission there was to explore the planet and its rings, moons, and magnetic field in greater detail than was possible for its predecessor, Pioneer 11. The spcaecraft found three new moons: Prometheus and Pandora, the "shepherding" moons that keep the F ring well-defined, and Altas which similrarly shepherds the A ring.

It is now exploring the outermost reaches of where the solar wind and the sun's magnetic field dominate space. In September 2007, it crossed the termination shock(where the speed of the solar wind drops below the speed of sound) at 84 AU(more than twice the distance to Pluto).   4. Cassini

    5. Cassini was designed to explore the Saturnian system from orbit: the planet and its atmosphere, rings and magnetosphere, and its moons, particularly Titan and the icy satelities. Cassini also carried Europe's Huygens probe to its rendzvous with Titan. Cassini completed its four-year primary mission in 2008 and went on to perform dozens more flybys of Titan, Enceladus and Saturn's other icy moons through its 10th anniversary in 2014.

    Here are a few of Cassini's accomplishments:

    • Titan revealed as an Earth-like world with rain, rivers, lakes and seas
    • First complete view of the north polar hexgon and discovery of giant hurricanes at both of Saturn's poles

It also carried cameras to photograph the moon's surface.
